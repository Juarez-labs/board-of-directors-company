## Macro Trends to Track

### 1. Agentic AI Adoption
The market is moving from single-model chat interfaces to multi-agent systems that can plan, execute, and iterate autonomously. Early adopters are software teams; the wave is expanding to operations, marketing, and finance.

### 2. AI Governance & Compliance
As AI agents take more autonomous actions, enterprises are demanding audit trails, access controls, and human-in-the-loop approval flows. Regulation is emerging (EU AI Act, US executive orders).

### 3. Developer-Led AI Tooling
The most successful AI tools are adopted bottom-up by developers before making their way to enterprise buyers. CLI-first, GitHub-native, and open-source-friendly products win early mindshare.

### 4. AI-Augmented Teams vs. AI Replacement
The dominant narrative is shifting from "AI replaces workers" to "AI-augmented teams outperform unaugmented ones." Buyers want to amplify their existing team, not eliminate it.

**Key signals:** [TBD]

### 5. Cost of Intelligence Dropping
Model API costs are falling rapidly. The bottleneck is shifting from "can we afford to run agents?" to "can we trust and coordinate agents reliably?"
