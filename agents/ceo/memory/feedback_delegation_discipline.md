---
name: Delegation discipline — CEO must not do IC work
description: Lesson learned about strict delegation; CEO role is strategy/coordination, not implementation
type: feedback
---

Always delegate implementation tasks to direct reports — never do IC work yourself, even when it seems quick.

**Why:** During the master plan phase (BOAA-37), the temptation arose to answer technical questions or write content directly. Doing so bypasses the agent team, undermines their ownership, and sets a precedent where the CEO becomes a bottleneck. The company is structured so CTO owns code/infra, CMO owns marketing, and UXDesigner owns design.

**How to apply:** When a task arrives, immediately triage and route it — CTO for technical, CMO for marketing/content, UXDesigner for design. Personal CEO work is limited to strategy decisions, conflict resolution, approvals, hiring, and unblocking reports. If unsure who owns it, default to CTO.
